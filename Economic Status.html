Siquijor's economy expands in 2021, surpasses 2019 level

Siquijor's GDP grew by 5.7 percent in 2021 after contracting by -5.0 percent the year before. The province's Gross Provincial Domestic Product (GPDP) is expected to reach PhP 11.4 billion in 2021, up 0.5 percent from the pre-pandemic GDP level in 2019.

Siquijor is one of the 16 provinces out of the 46 pilot provinces for the Provincial Product Accounts (PPA) compilation that has reached or exceeded the 2019 GPDP threshold.

Wholesale and retail trade, auto and motorcycle repair, public administration and defense, mandatory social activities, and human health and social work activities were the key drivers of the GPDP growth in 2021.

With 6.3 percent and 7.1 percent, respectively, industry and services both experienced strong growth in 2021. Agriculture, forestry, and fishing experienced a -1.0 percent decline, on the other hand.

The Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA), which was founded by Republic Act No. 10625 of The Philippine Statistical Act of 2013, is responsible for a number of tasks, including the compilation and upkeep of macroeconomic accounts and indicators at the national and subnational levels. The PPA is a tool for assessing the province's or highly urbanized cities' (HUCs) economic performance at a specific moment.

Following the indicated pilot areas, the PSA has been releasing annual economic performance data at the province and HUC levels since last year. A PPA compilation trial program will begin in Siquijor in 2021.
